Yellow

Yellow gold gets its color from a blend of color-saturated alloys and, of course, pure gold. Copper (red) and Silver (green) are mixed with 14K or 18K gold to give our signature gold it’s warm glow.

Worth our Weight

We ethically source, sustainably make, and only use real, solid 18-karat and 14-karat gold in our jewelry. Measured in 24ths, 18-karats denotes 18/24 parts of gold while 14-karats denotes 14/24 parts of pure gold. The remaining percentage is an alloy added to enhance durability of naturally soft gold, allowing us to guarantee our gold for life.

14K Solid Gold

14K gold denotes 58.3% gold mixed with alloys to make it ideal for fine jewelry and strong enough for everyday wear. Accessible without sacrificing quality, 14K is the perfect happy medium.

18K Solid Gold

While pure gold is too soft for fine jewelry, our 18K gold, made with 75% gold mixed with alloys, is the closest you can get to 100% gold while maintaining strength, resilience, and shine.

C for yourself

When it comes to diamonds, we stick to the Four C’s — Carat, Color, Cut, and Clarity. All of our diamonds are colorless (F-D), graded ‘Very Good’ or better in cut, and SI or higher in clarity. Carat varies depending on piece, but we generally believe the bigger the better.

To Clean

Mix a few drops of detergent-free soap with warm water. Soak your jewelry, one piece at a time, and brush lightly to clean each piece. Rinse with care, lightly dry, and continue wearing proudly.

Our gold is strong but sensitive, just like you. We design and test every piece to withstand the normal rigors of everyday wear, but also to be handled with care.

How to measure

1

Tear up a strip of paper (Book pages are vetoed. You know how much we love them).

2

Wrap the paper snug around your finger. Make sure it’s nice and tight, then mark the spot where it overlaps.

3

Measure the distance with a ruler (in mm); that’s your finger’s circumference.

What’s your size?

Pro tips for measuring

Avoid Swelling. Measure at the end of day.

Thicker bands can feel tight. Consider sizing up.

Cold hands shrink. Make sure your hands are warm when measuring.
